Summary

  • SYGNAL Analysis of the patient’s molecular profiling data matched to 147 regulons in the mmSYGNAL model with 133 OVERACTIVE regulons (filtered for Drug constrained regulon activity > 0.8).

  • 6 of these regulons were OVERACTIVE for 1 Glioblastoma SOC drug(s) that included 1 target(s).

  • 13 of these regulons were OVERACTIVE for 1 drug(s) that are in Clinical Trials for GBM that included 1 target(s).

  • 133 of these regulons were OVERACTIVE for 34 drug(s) that are FDA Approved for other indications that included 21 target(s).

  • DNA sequence analysis showed that patient has 79 mutations in 66 unique genes with frequency greater than 20%.

  • Of these mutations 4 identified as cancer driver mutations based on the analysis performed through ‘Cancer Genome Interpreter’. In addition, 3 of these mutations were alterations described as biomarkers for a different tumor type as interpreted by Cancer Genome Interpreter.

Short description of fields

  • Target: Drug target from the network that is predicted to be associated with disease progression.
  • Drug(s) that are associated with given target
  • Drug Mechanism: Mechanism of the drug. If there are more than one drug, multiple mechanisms are collapsed with ':'
  • Regulon activity: Measure of how active is the regulon(s) that are associated with the target in the patient's tumor.
  • Regulon Activity Summary: Count of Overactive(O), Underactive(U) or All (A) regulons in the patient.
  • GBM Trials: How many trials match to listed drugs in GBM.
  • Literature Evidence: Number of curated literature evidence for the target and GBM. Click on the target name to get more details.

Patient Mutations

### Mutations identified as driver(s)

The interpretation of the patients mutations was performed through a report obtained from Cancer Genome Interpreter database. Cancer Genome Interpreter annotates the biological and clinical relevance of tumor alterations. For more information see Tamborero et al.,

BOOSTDM_DS/Oncogenicity

  • Driver according to BoostDM tissue specific model: a model built from mutations observed in this gene in this tumor type.
  • Driver according to BoostDM non tissue specific model: a model built from mutations observed in this gene in other tumor type.
  • Driver according to OncodriveMUT: there is no tissue specific or non-specific model to evaluate this mutation or indel; hence it is classified as driver through a set of rules implemented by OncodriveMUT.
  • Passenger: Classified as passenger through a BoostDM model or OncodriveMUT
  • Non-affecting: Non-protein affecting mutation.

Alterations described as biomarkers

A biomarkers report as obtained from Cancer Genome Interpreter database. It contains the putative biomarkers of drug response found in the tumor organized according to distinct levels of clinical relevance.

Response

  • Responsive: the genomic alteration is associated to sensitivity to the drug
  • No responsive: the genomic alteration is not associated to sensitivity to the drug
  • Resistant: the genomic alteration is associated to resistance to the drug
  • Increased toxicity: the genomic alteration enhances the toxicity related to the use of the drug

Evidence

  • Level A: corresponds to biomarkers used in professional guidelines of FDA approved drugs.
  • Level B: groups biomarkers observed in clinical trials.
  • Level C: corresponds to biomarkers identified from small group studies or case studies.
  • Level D: biomarkers have been identified in pre-clinical studies.
